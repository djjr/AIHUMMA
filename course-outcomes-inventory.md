## Taxonomy Reference

### Cluster TL: Technical Literacy

- **TL-01:** Explain how core ML model types (classification, regression, clustering, deep learning, generative models) work at a conceptual level
- **TL-02:** Interpret technical AI documentation (model cards, capability evaluations, technical specs) as arguments rather than facts
- **TL-03:** Implement basic ML workflows using Python and standard libraries (scikit-learn, PyTorch, TensorFlow)
- **TL-04:** Explain LLM architecture and training processes (attention mechanisms, transformers, RLHF, fine-tuning, prompt engineering)
- **TL-05:** Apply mathematical foundations (linear algebra, probability, calculus, optimization) to understand ML system design
- **TL-06:** Distinguish among AI paradigms (rule-based systems, narrow ML, LLMs, generative AI, agentic AI) and their capabilities and limits
- **TL-07:** Evaluate AI system performance, failure modes, and deployment constraints using appropriate frameworks

### Cluster CH: Critical & Humanistic Analysis

- **CH-01:** Situate AI development within its intellectual and political history (from 1940s to present)
- **CH-02:** Analyze AI discourse, artifacts, and promotional claims as ideologically and rhetorically constructed texts
- **CH-03:** Apply humanistic methods (close reading, ethnography, cultural analysis, oral history) to study AI phenomena
- **CH-04:** Critique AI representations in cultural forms (film, theatre, fiction, media) and what they reveal about social anxieties
- **CH-05:** Evaluate AI claims about consciousness, intelligence, and agency using philosophical and cognitive science frameworks
- **CH-06:** Analyze AI through, for example, feminist, postcolonial, critical race, or disability frameworks to reveal differential impacts

### Cluster EV: Ethics & Values

- **EV-01:** Apply major ethical frameworks (consequentialism, rights-based, virtue ethics, care ethics) to AI deployment decisions
- **EV-02:** Identify and analyze sources of algorithmic bias and unfairness across different social domains
- **EV-03:** Evaluate responsible AI instruments (ethics guidelines, model cards, impact assessments, audit frameworks) for their scope and limits
- **EV-04:** Analyze the ethics of AI in high-stakes domains (healthcare, criminal justice, hiring, surveillance, warfare)
- **EV-05:** Distinguish procedural ethics from structural/systemic critique and recognize when ethics discourse substitutes for governance
- **EV-06:** Assess privacy, consent, and data rights implications of AI systems and their deployment contexts

### Cluster GP: Governance & Policy

- **GP-01:** Compare AI regulatory frameworks across jurisdictions (EU AI Act, US, UK, China, Global South)
- **GP-02:** Apply institutional design and political economy analysis to AI governance challenges
- **GP-03:** Draw on historical analogies (nuclear, pharma, aviation, finance) to analyze AI governance options and their limitations
- **GP-04:** Evaluate AI in public sector, national security, and government contexts, including autonomous systems and intelligence analysis
- **GP-05:** Assess technical AI safety proposals (RLHF, red-teaming, evals, constitutional AI) from a policy and governance standpoint
- **GP-06:** Analyze international AI competition and cooperation, including US-China dynamics and global governance institutions

### Cluster RM: Research & Analytical Methods

- **RM-01:** Design and conduct qualitative research (ethnographic fieldwork, elite interviewing, document analysis, discourse analysis)
- **RM-02:** Design and conduct computational or quantitative research on AI-related phenomena
- **RM-03:** Critically evaluate academic and policy research, distinguishing strong evidence from weak claims
- **RM-04:** Synthesize evidence across multiple sources to construct well-supported analytical arguments
- **RM-05:** Use AI tools as research instruments (for literature synthesis, data analysis, drafting, and ideation) with critical awareness

### Cluster CW: Communication & Writing

- **CW-01:** Produce policy genre writing (memos, briefs, white papers, testimony) calibrated to non-specialist decision-makers
- **CW-02:** Write data-driven narratives for general audiences (journalism, public essays, science writing)
- **CW-03:** Translate technical AI concepts clearly for lay or policy audiences in written and oral form
- **CW-04:** Produce persuasive arguments in multiple genres calibrated to specific audiences and institutional contexts
- **CW-05:** Use generative AI tools to enhance and accelerate writing, summarization, and communication workflows
- **CW-06:** Direct and evaluate AI-assisted writing for policy quality — applying expert judgment to AI-assisted workflows, evaluating output against professional standards, recognizing plausible-but-wrong analysis, and maintaining author accountability for what goes out under your name

### Cluster HI: Human-AI Interaction & Design

- **HI-01:** Apply human-centered design principles to evaluate or build AI-enabled systems and interfaces
- **HI-02:** Design for AI explainability, user trust, and appropriate human oversight in AI deployments
- **HI-03:** Analyze augmentation vs. replacement dynamics in human-AI collaborative systems
- **HI-04:** Evaluate human-AI teaming structures, their effects on agency, accountability, and decision quality
- **HI-05:** Assess emerging human-AI modalities (brain-computer interfaces, wearable biometrics, agentic AI) and their social implications

### Cluster SD: Societal & Domain Applications

- **SD-01:** Evaluate AI applications in healthcare (diagnostics, clinical decision support, equity in access, regulatory approval)
- **SD-02:** Analyze AI in national security contexts (autonomous weapons, intelligence analysis, cyber operations, disinformation)
- **SD-03:** Assess AI's impact on labor markets, employment structures, migration, and economic inequality
- **SD-04:** Examine AI's role in democracy, political discourse, media ecosystems, and institutional stability
- **SD-05:** Apply AI methods and tools in creative domains (music, film, performance, interactive media)
- **SD-06:** Evaluate AI in educational settings (pedagogy design, learning analytics, curriculum development, academic integrity)
- **SD-07:** Analyze AI applications in business and marketing contexts (strategy, customer analytics, operations)

**Outcome Codes:** TL-01, TL-05, CH-04, CH-05, EV-01, EV-02, EV-04

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Students will be able to characterize machine learning approaches and evaluate their applicability to problems in this domain.
- Students will be able to apply mathematical and probabilistic reasoning to understand machine learning system design and limitations.
- Students will be able to analyze how AI is represented in cultural media and what these representations reveal about social values and anxieties.

### How to Pay Attention and Why · AS.300.615
**School/Unit:** KSAS
**Tech↔Hum:** 5/5 · **Primary Clusters:** Technical Literacy, Critical & Humanistic Analysis, Ethics & Values, Governance & Policy

**Outcome Codes:** TL-04, CH-02, CH-03, CH-04, CH-06, EV-03, GP-02

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Students will be able to apply close reading and textual analysis methods to interpret AI-related discourse, promotional materials, and technical documentation.
- Students will be able to critique the rhetorical and ideological construction of claims about AI capabilities and intelligence.
- Students will be able to design and conduct discourse analysis studying how AI is represented and discussed across media contexts.
- Students will be able to synthesize media studies, humanistic scholarship, and critical theory to analyze the attention economy and AI's role in it.

**Signature Capabilities:**
- Can conduct close reading of AI-related texts (research papers, product marketing, media coverage) to deconstruct implicit claims and ideological framings.
- Can produce a substantive analysis of how AI is represented in a particular media domain (journalism, advertising, entertainment) and what it reveals about social anxieties and preoccupations.
