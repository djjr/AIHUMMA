# Maryland Competitive Landscape: AI, Humanities, and Governance Programs

*Research conducted April 2026*

## Summary Finding

No Maryland institution occupies the same white space as the proposed AIHUMMA program. There is no Maryland program that (a) takes humanities and social science graduates as its primary population, (b) builds intellectual community and portfolio around AI's meaning and governance, and (c) targets the civil society/NGO/research sector rather than technical roles or federal/diplomatic careers.

---

## Programs with Real, If Partial, Overlap

### Johns Hopkins SAIS — Technology and Innovation Concentration
*Within the MA in International Relations*

The closest structural neighbor in Maryland. The MAIR's Technology and Innovation concentration explicitly addresses AI governance, cyber, and synthetic biology through a policy-and-society lens, and students engage with ethical and geopolitical dimensions of emerging technology. An Emerging Technologies Initiative (launched 2023) brings together JHU and D.C.-area experts for student programming on AI governance and related topics.

**Why it's different:** The MAIR is a broad international relations degree training future diplomats and IR generalists — not specialist humanists. The student population, career arc, and institutional framing differ substantially. AI governance is one theme within a wider international affairs education.

- [SAIS Technology and Innovation](https://sais.jhu.edu/academics/master-degrees/master-arts-international-relations-mair/technology-and-innovation)
- [JHU Emerging Tech Initiative](https://emergingtech.sais.jhu.edu/about)

---

### UMD School of Public Policy — MPP with Science & Technology Policy Track

A generalist public policy degree (48 credits) that lists S&T policy as one of several concentration areas. The school has also been building AI-adjacent programming, including an "AI in Government" curriculum initiative and a STEP (Science, Technology, Ethics and Policy) interdisciplinary minor co-sponsored with the College of Information Studies and the Clark School of Engineering.

**Why it's different:** The MPP trains public administrators and policy analysts broadly. No dedicated humanities framing, no specialist AI governance depth, and no particular orientation toward civil society or NGO careers. The STEP minor is undergraduate/cross-listed and currently oversubscribed.

- [UMD MPP](https://spp.umd.edu/your-education/masters/master-public-policy)
- [STEP Minor](https://spp.umd.edu/science-technology-ethics-and-policy-step-minor)

---

### UMBC — MS in Human-Centered Computing

The most intellectually adjacent program at a non-flagship Maryland institution. HCC addresses technology design through a social and ethical lens, drawing on HCI, usability, and accessibility frameworks. Offers both a practitioner track and a research/thesis track. Has been running since 2007.

**Why it's different:** Housed in Information Systems and fundamentally computing-adjacent — admission assumes technical fluency, and career outputs are UX directors and HCI researchers, not governance or policy professionals.

- [UMBC HCC](https://informationsystems.umbc.edu/home/graduate-programs/master-of-science-programs/master-of-science-in-human-centered-computing-hcc/)

## Programs That Sound Similar but Aren't

### UMD MS in Artificial Intelligence (MSAI)
30 credits, non-thesis, housed in the College of Computer, Mathematical, and Natural Sciences. Includes a course called "AI Policy" and mentions ethics as a theme — but admission requires calculus II, linear algebra, statistics, and programming fluency. This is an engineering-adjacent MSAI with some humanities-inflected course offerings. Precisely the type of "technically-anchored degree disguised as a policy program" that AIHUMMA's positioning explicitly contrasts against.

- [UMD MSAI](https://cmns.umd.edu/graduate/science-academy/artificial-intelligence)

---

### Capitol Technology University — MPhil in Artificial Intelligence
A research-focused degree centered on machine learning, deep learning, and NLP, concluding in a dissertation. Serves working professionals with existing AI/technical backgrounds. No policy or governance orientation.

- [CapTech MPhil AI](https://www.captechu.edu/degrees-and-programs/masters-degrees/artificial-intelligence-mphil)

---

### University of Baltimore — MS in Interaction Design and Information Architecture
Focused on human-information design and technology application. Marginally relevant; no governance or policy orientation.

## Competitive Positioning Implication

The Maryland landscape confirms the white-space argument. SAIS's Technology and Innovation track is the only program a prospective student might compare to AIHUMMA, but the MAIR's breadth, IR orientation, Washington D.C. location, and price point make it a genuinely different proposition — and one that serves a different student type.
