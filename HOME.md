#AIHUMMA 
**ABOUT:** This is an experimental collaboration platform for exploring Claude Cowork and Code workflows for curriculum and program development. Created by Dan Ryan March 2026.

### Ideas
We imagine our brief is to create a masters degree targeted at humanities and social science bachelors who want to work in the AI space in ways that build on their backgrounds (as opposed to making a career shift to technology).
#### How did we get here?
Think about pathways. A program takes students from point A to point B. What are some? 
- bridge for humanities and social science students to pursue advanced STEM training akin to post-bacc pre-med programs
- bridge for highly accomplished students of humanities and social sciences to employment in NGOs, government, and private sector around AI policy (writ large)
- bridge for highly accomplished students of humanities and social sciences to become humanities and social science scholars who focus on AI-related topics
Possible Intellectual Focus

	 - History of technology risk and governance methods
	 - Political Science / History / Sociology debates about innovation, regulation, governance
- What's at Stake - Humanism in the Face of Human Exceeding Technology?
