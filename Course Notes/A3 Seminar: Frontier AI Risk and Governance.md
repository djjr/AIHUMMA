**3 credits | Track A: Policy and Governance Research**

The most intellectually demanding course in the program. Reads the serious literature on catastrophic and transformative AI risk: Ord, Bostrom, Christiano, Davidson, Yudkowsky (as a primary source, read critically), and the empirical evaluation literature from METR and Apollo Research. Also reads the critics: Gebru on AI hype, Gary Marcus on capability claims, Kate Crawford on power concentration. Students are expected to form defensible views and argue for them. The goal is a student who can engage with METR researchers or AI Now researchers with equal fluency — who has read both literatures and can say something substantive about where they agree, where they conflict, and what governance implications follow from each.
